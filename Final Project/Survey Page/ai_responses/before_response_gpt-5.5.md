## Summary

The page has major accessibility barriers across perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust requirements. The most significant problems are unlabeled and incorrectly grouped form controls, inaccessible keyboard and focus behavior, missing or incorrect text alternatives, poor document structure caused by layout tables and non-semantic markup, insufficient contrast, unclear links, and unpredictable behavior when controls receive focus or change value. These issues substantially affect screen reader users, keyboard-only users, users with low vision, users with cognitive disabilities, and users of assistive technologies.

## Strengths

- The page contains mostly text-based content rather than requiring audio or video.
- No obvious flashing content was detected.
- Some native HTML form controls are present, which could be made accessible with proper labeling, grouping, and focus handling.
- There is visible survey content and a results section that can be made accessible with semantic markup.

---

## Top 3 Priority Fixes

1. Rebuild the survey form with semantic HTML: use fieldset and legend for radio groups, label elements for every input/select, correct reading order, clear required-field instructions, and proper accessible names.
2. Fix keyboard accessibility and focus management: remove scripts that blur focus, ensure all interactive controls are keyboard operable, preserve a logical focus order, and provide a clearly visible focus indicator.
3. Replace layout/image-based structure with semantic HTML: use real headings, lists, tables for tabular data, meaningful links, accurate alt text or empty alt for decorative images, and sufficient color contrast.
